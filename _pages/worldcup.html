World Cup

Objective

I wanted to create an appealing, responsive website to allow people to easily explore the teams and matches in the Fifa World Cup 2018. Also, to make the data available through a REST API for developers to use in whatever maniacal way they think of!

The official Fifa website has most of the features I would like to implement but it is lacking in some areas, so I will base my design on their website, but make some changes.

Finished Application

The finished application is hosted here on heroku. Because it is hosted using a free account, the performance is limited. The website sleeps after 30 minutes of inactivity. So, it may take 2 minutes for the website to be appear for you.

Requirements

  1. Homepage: The Fifa homepage looks poor on smaller screens, the navigation is obscured with a scrollbar. A sticky navigation bar that turns into a dropdown that can be toggled is preferable.
  2. Homepage: The next 4 matches are shown on the homepage, which again looks poor on mobile. I would like to show the next 2 games, and stack them vertically on mobile.
  3. Matches: Fifa shows a long list grouped by stage("Knockout Phase" by default), and ordered by date (30 Jun and ascending). I would prefer to see the upcoming matches on top, the next 1-4 matches (depending on the stage). It can be clicked on to show all upcoming games within a complete list.
  4. Matches: The complete list can be grouped by "all", "results", and "upcoming".
  5. Matches: The complete list can be filtered by "team".
  6. Players: Fifa shows a picture of the player with his team and position. I would like to show this in a similar way but display some information when you hover over the player.
  7. Players: When you browse all of the players, it loads images for every single player, lazy loading of images would be preferable.
  8. Players: Filter by "team" and "position".
  9. Players: Clicking on the player will show a profile page featuring their key stats.
  10. Teams: Fifa does a good job. I would prefer to just have country flag as a card and show their ranking.
  11. Teams: Clicking on the team will show a profile page with key stats.
  12. Stadiums: Show the stadiums as an icon on a map, with each scaled by capacity.
  13. Stadiums: The data of the stadium will be show as a companion table when you click on the icon
  14. Dream team: Pick a dream team from any of the players.
  15. Dream team: Choose a formation and show players on the pitch as a portait photo with their name.
  16. Dream team: Choose a formation and show players on the pitch as a portait photo with their name.
  17. Dream team: You can see a player's key stats on hover.
  18. Dream team: You can auto-complete the team.
  19. Dream team: You can clear all players.
  20. Developer API: Shows all of the endpoints available grouped by resource, and styled similarly to most professional web services.
