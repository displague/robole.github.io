Things that are absent, or not that apparent from guidebooks

  1. How relentless Morrocans are when it comes to getting money from you somehow. People will follow you trying to be a guide, outright con-artists, salesmen, and beggars. It's hard to have a peaceful time walking around, but if you are good-natured about it, some will switch and have a regular conversation. Other times, you may pay a small amount to be left in peace!
  2. Reviews of accomodation are overwhelming positive. Generally, Moroccan staff are extremely friendly and the old buildings are beautiful, this skews people's feedback and can be misleading. Especially in Marrakech, which seems to be where most people begin their trip. The hostel I was in for 1 night in Marrakech was noisy and had 2 bathrooms for a lot of guests, this was not mentioned in any reviews. Prices are negotiable most of the time.
  3. No idea how to get a shared cab, couldn't get a fair price when getting a cab. Not many bus-stops or information how to use local transport!
  4. Train service is good. Ticket machines in station tend to breakdown a lot. Some routes are busier than others and you should try to board early, seating is not assigned in second class.
  5. Difficult to pay local prices in restaurants. Not everywhere has menus, and if they do, prices are higher for the english menus. Speaking a bit more French can help to combat this by asking for prices before ordering.
  6. YHA hostels in Casablanca and Rabat are not on internet
  7. Trustworthiness of local car rental agencies is hard to verify online. I got a brand new car from Medloc and it was a straightforward transaction, and was 40% cheaper than the international companies. I was able to drop the car back out of office hours, and the staff member dropped me to my hotel as well!
  8. Driving to Dades Gorge, I had no idea how far I had to go to see the deepest, most dramatic vista of the gorge.
  9. Kasbah Amridil has a fake counterpart, they built a new kasbah right next to the original to look similar, and they charge more. On arrival, staff will persaude you to come into the fake! This is mentioned in the Lonely Planet, but I didn't know about it until I arrived, and was fortunate to find the real one when I was walking around ignoring the men asking me to come in!
  10. A price guide for restraurants would be helpful. There are always cheaper away from tourist hotspots (medinas), and often food is tastier and more authentic.
  11. It would be great to have a collection of photos for each attraction and the city to decide where to visit. This would have been helpful in choosing between the movie studios in Ouarzazate, I didn't have time to go to both.
  12. Marrakech airport is very modern and pleasant, but it takes some time to get to departure gate because:
    • You must show passport and have luggage scanned at entrance to terminal building
    • It was required by TAP Airlines to verify your credit card in person. So I had to go to another desk to do this, and then return to the checkin desk. Even if you check in online, you need to go to the desk to verify your carry-on luggage to proceed through security.
    • It's not possible to exchange Moroccan Dhirams outside of the country. So, you need to exchange your leftover currency before going to security
    • Standard security screening, and passport control before you reach the departure gates.
