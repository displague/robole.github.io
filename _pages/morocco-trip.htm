Things that are absent, or not that apparent from guidebooks

  • How relentless Morrocans are when it comes to getting money from you somehow. People will follow you trying to be a guide, outright con-artists, salesmen, and beggars. It's hard to have a peaceful time walking around, but if you are good-natured about it, some will switch and have a regular conversation. Other times, you may pay a small amount to be left in peace!
  • Reviews of accomodation are overwhelming positive. Generally, Moroccan staff are extremely friendly and the old buildings are beautiful, this skews people's feedback and can be misleading. Especially in Marrakech, which seems to be where most people begin their trip. Prices are negotiable.
  • No idea how to get a shared cab, couldn't get a fair price when getting a cab. Not many bus-stops or information how to use local transport!
  • Train service is good. Ticket machines in station tend to breakdown a lot. Some routes are busier than others and you should try to board early, seating is not assigned in second class.
  • Difficult to pay local prices in restaurants. Not everywhere has menus, and if they do, prices are higher for the english menus. Speaking a bit more French can help to combat this by asking for prices before ordering.
  • YHA hostels in Casablanca and Rabat are not on internet
  • Trustworthiness of local car rental agencies is hard to verify online. I got a brand new car from Medloc and it was a straightforward transaction. I was able to drop the car back out of office hours, and the staff member dropped me off as well!
  • Driving to Dades Gorge, I had no idea how far I had to go to see the most deepest, most dramatic vista of the gorge.
  • Kasbah Amridil has a fake entrance (it is mentioned in the Lonely Planet).
  • Price guide for each city would help to see differences. Always cheaper away from tourist hotspots (medinas).
